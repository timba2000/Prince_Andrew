# SS Yongala Wreck: A Majestic Underwater Mausoleum

Dive into history and vibrant marine life at the SS Yongala Wreck, consistently ranked among the world's top wreck dives. Sinking dramatically in 1911 after encountering a cyclone, this 109-meter passenger and freight steamer now rests majestically on the seabed, forming an artificial reef that pulsates with an astonishing array of marine biodiversity. Located off the coast of Townsville, Queensland, the Yongala is a protected historic site, ensuring its pristine condition and the thriving ecosystem it supports. This dive offers an unparalleled experience for advanced divers seeking both historical intrigue and spectacular encounters with large pelagic species and massive schools of fish.

**The Essentials:**
*   **Depth Range:** 14-30m. The wreck's highest point is at 14m, while the deepest accessible parts reach 30m.
*   **Visibility:** Typically 10-30m. Visibility is generally best during the dry season (May to November), offering clear views of the entire wreck. During the wet/cyclone season (January-April), visibility can be reduced to 10-15m due to increased runoff and plankton blooms, though this can also attract large schools of bait fish.
*   **Water Temperature:** Ranging from 22-28°C, making for comfortable diving conditions year-round.
*   **Current/Conditions:** Can experience moderate to strong currents, requiring experienced divers. Divers descend using a mooring line. Due to its exposure, conditions can change rapidly.
*   **Viz Implications:** The presence of bait fish in the wet season can significantly impact visibility, creating a dense, living curtain around the wreck.

**Terrain & Navigation:**
The SS Yongala lies on its starboard side, largely intact, making it a truly immersive wreck dive. Its sheer size allows for multiple dives to fully explore its features. Key areas include the engine room, cargo holds, and bow section. Due to regulations protecting the wreck, penetration is generally not permitted, but the exterior offers an abundance of structures to explore, covered in corals and sponges. Divers typically follow a guide to navigate the expansive site and avoid entanglement.

**Marine Life Highlights:**
The Yongala is a magnet for marine life, boasting incredible biodiversity. Expect encounters with formidable bull sharks, graceful eagle rays, and numerous species of sea snakes. Giant Queensland groupers, often as large as a diver, are a common sight, as are massive schools of trevally, barracuda, and snappers. Look out for marble rays, various species of turtles, and vibrant nudibranchs that inhabit the wreck's crevices. The sheer density of marine life here is astounding, transforming the wreck into a dynamic, living reef.

**Logistics & Facilities:**
Access to the SS Yongala is exclusively via day boat trips departing from Townsville or Ayr. Due to the site's advanced nature and potential currents, divers are required to hold an Advanced Open Water certification or higher. Operators typically provide full gear hire and guided dives. Conservation is paramount; strict guidelines are in place to protect the wreck and its inhabitants, including no touching, no taking of souvenirs, and adherence to buoyancy control.

**Who Is It For?:**
This dive is strictly for Advanced Open Water divers with a good level of experience in various conditions, comfortable with potential currents, and excellent buoyancy control. It is not suitable for novice divers or those uncomfortable in open ocean conditions.

**Pro-Tip:**
Book your trip during the dry season (May to November) for the best visibility, but don't discount the wet season if you want to experience the thrill of massive bait ball formations and the pelagic action they attract!
